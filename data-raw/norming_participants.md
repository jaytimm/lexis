## Dataset Notes

### Age of Acquisition — Kuperman, Stadthagen-Gonzalez, & Brysbaert (2012)
- **N:** 1,960 responders; 1,729 retained after exclusions (88%)
- **Platform:** Amazon Mechanical Turk; US residents only; no English L1 requirement
- **Age:** range 15–82; 47% aged 20–29; 8% under 20; 11% over 49; 1,136 female, 593 male
- **Country:** USA
- **Pay:** $1.81 per completed list (~300 words, ~20 min)

### Body-Object Interaction — Pexman et al. (2019)
- **N:** 1,258 initial (708 male, 550 female); 743 final after exclusions (366 female, 377 male)
- **Platform:** Amazon Mechanical Turk; all participants over 18; English as first language required (157 excluded)
- **Age:** M = 37.37 (SD = 11.08), all 18+
- **Country:** Not reported. No country restriction was stated; English L1 required. MTurk samples of this era were predominantly U.S.-based.
- **Pay:** US$2 per list

### Concreteness — Brysbaert, Warriner, & Kuperman (2014)
- **N:** 4,237 workers; 2,385,204 total ratings; ~25 raters per word target
- **Platform:** Amazon Mechanical Turk; restricted to self-identified US residents
- **Age:** 36% typical student age (17–25); 1% older than 65; 57% female; 149 nonnative English speakers removed
- **Country:** USA
- **Pay:** 75 cents per completed list (~14 min)

### Gender Connotation — Roberts & Utych (2020)
- **N:** 175 MTurk participants; each participant rated ~100 words; 15–44 raters per word (M = 25, SD = 5.19)
- **Platform:** Amazon Mechanical Turk (June 2018)
- **Age:** not reported
- **Country:** Not reported. No country restriction was stated. MTurk samples of this era were predominantly U.S.-based.
- **Pay:** $1 per ~6 min task

### Glasgow Norms — Scott et al. (2019)
- **N:** 829 unique participants (1,368 total including multiple-list completions); 599 female (72%), 230 male (28%)
- **Platform:** Online via in-house University of Glasgow platform; recruited via Psychology department advertisement
- **Age:** M = 21.7 (SD = 7.4), range 16–73 years
- **Country:** UK; native English speakers from University of Glasgow community
- **Pay:** £6/h or course credit; study conformed to British Psychological Society ethical guidelines

### Humor — Engelthaler & Hills (2018)
- **N:** 821 final (950 initial; 102 removed for incomplete/errors, 27 for low variability); 478 female (58%), 341 male (42%)
- **Platform:** Amazon Mechanical Turk; any registered member; participants rated 211 words
- **Age:** M = 35.37 (SD = 11.74), range 18–78
- **Country:** Not reported. No geographic restriction was stated. MTurk samples of this era were predominantly U.S.-based.
- **Pay:** US$1 per ~15 min task

### Iconicity — Winter et al. (2024)
- **N:** 1,419 American English speakers (95.7% native English; 51.8% female, 41.0% male, 0.5% other, 6.8% unreported)
- **Platform:** 55% Amazon Mechanical Turk; 43% University of Wisconsin–Madison Psychology participant pool
- **Age:** M = 30 (SD = 14), range 18–88
- **Country:** USA
- **Pay:** $0.60 per 50-word set (MTurk); course credit (UW-Madison)
- **Data collection:** April 2020 – September 2021, with additional collection through September 2022

### Imageability — Bird, Franklin, & Howard (2001)
- **N:** 78 subjects for imageability (M_age = 65.0, SD = 18.7); 45 subjects for AoA (M = 60.7, SD = 15.5)
- **Platform:** Volunteers drawn from a panel previously used for aphasia research (adults aged 50–80); plus some Newcastle undergraduates (~20–40)
- **Age:** M ≈ 65; predominantly 50–80 age range; wide spread
- **Country:** UK; native speakers of British English; Newcastle upon Tyne
- **Note:** Volunteer (non-paid) participation; ratio of female to male approximately 2:1

### Lancaster Sensorimotor Norms — Lynott et al. (2020)
- **N:** 3,500 unique participants (perceptual strength component: 2,625; action strength: 1,933)
- **Platform:** Amazon Mechanical Turk; experienced MTurk users (>100 HITs, >97% approval rate); English L1 required
- **Age:** M = 34.9 (SD = 10.3); perceptual: M = 35.0; action: M = 35.7; ~47% female
- **Country:** Not reported. No country restriction was stated; English L1 required; ethics approved by Lancaster University. MTurk samples of this era were predominantly U.S.-based.
- **Pay:** US$2.75 per perceptual list; US$2.25 per action list (~18 and 15 min respectively)

### Lexical Decision (ELP) — Balota et al. (2007)
- **N:** 816 participants for lexical decision; 444 for speeded naming; all native English speakers
- **Platform:** Six US universities (Washington Univ. in St. Louis, Morehead State, SUNY Albany, Univ. of Kansas, Univ. of South Florida, Wayne State); paid $25 or course credit
- **Age:** M = 22.86 (SD = 6.85) for lexical decision; M = 23.51 (SD = 9.31) for naming
- **Country:** USA (Midwest, Northeast, Southeast institutions)
- **Note:** ~3,400 observations per participant; each participant saw ~2,500 (naming) to 3,500 (LD) stimuli across 2 sessions

### Word Prevalence — Brysbaert et al. (2019)
- **N:** 221,268 individuals who completed 265,346 sessions
- **Platform:** Open-access online vocabulary test (http://vocabulary.ugent.be/); unrestricted access; no recruitment
- **Age:** not reported
- **Country:** USA and UK (restricted analyses to first three tests from native English speakers from US and UK); 56% female, 44% male
- **Note:** Largest participant pool of any dataset in lexis by far; test was self-administered and public

### Sensory Experience — Juhasz & Yap (2013)
- **N:** 63 native English speakers (for the 3,000 disyllabic words); monosyllabic norms from Juhasz et al. (2011)
- **Platform:** Wesleyan University undergraduates; rated words for course credit
- **Age:** not reported (college undergraduates, typically 18–22)
- **Country:** USA (Wesleyan University, Middletown, CT)
- **Note:** Paper-and-pencil questionnaire format; 10–11 participants per word; untimed

### Socialness — Diveica, Pexman, & Binney (2023)
- **N:** 605 initial (359 male, 240 female, 6 unspecified); 539 final after exclusions (317 male [58.8%], 216 female [40.1%], 6 unspecified)
- **Platform:** Prolific; restricted to fluent English speakers with no language disorders
- **Age:** M = 29.44 (SD = 10.6) initial; M = 29.7 (SD = 10.67) final; range 18–76
- **Country:** UK (paid GBP £4; ~34 min task); English was first language for 50.65%; 65.68% reported speaking more than one language
- **Note:** Multilingual, diverse sample; English fluency (not L1) was the criterion

### Valence / Arousal / Dominance — Warriner, Kuperman, & Brysbaert (2013)
- **N:** 1,827 contributors (723 valence, 745 arousal, 845 dominance; some contributed to >1 dimension); ~60% female across all three
- **Platform:** Amazon Mechanical Turk; restricted to self-identified US residents; participants completed only one list
- **Age:** range 16–87; 11% ≤ 20; 45% aged 21–30; 21% aged 31–40
- **Country:** USA
- **Pay:** 75 cents per ~14 min list (350 words)
- **Data collection:** March 14 – May 30, 2012

### Verbs in Space — Meteyard & Vigliocco (2009)
- **N:** ~96 native English speakers for main set of 295 verbs (32 raters per verb); 15 additional for 21 supplementary verbs
- **Platform:** Undergraduate students at University College London
- **Age:** not reported; predominantly undergraduate age (18–25); described as "predominantly British or American"
- **Country:** UK (University College London); Western/European background
